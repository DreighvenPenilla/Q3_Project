What is Fire Prevention Month in the Philippines?


According to Presidential Proclamation No. 115-A, s.1966, March is proclaimed as Fire Prevention Month for the Philippines. This is a time where various individuals are reminded to be aware of numerous fire-causing hazards that could be found in their homes. Additionally, March is said to be one of the warmest months for our country, not only because of the fact it's the start of summer, but also because March is also the month wherein there tends to be various reported cases of fires happening in the country.
What is the Importance of This?
To maintain the peace within our residences and to prevent fatal dangers that may kill many, we are required to be aware of the things in our environment that can cause fires. Fire does not only hurt us when we come into close contact with it, but it also causes dangers with the smoke we inhale. This smoke contains harmful chemicals which may lead to infections or diseases in the circulatory and respiratory system.
Statistics
Recently, the FDP had reported that there have been 1.9k fire incidents this year. The cost for the damages in these incidents had risen by 52.3% compared to last year, from PHP 942M to PHP 1.4B. 196 cases of injured civilians in these incidents were reported which was slightly higher than the cases last year in the same time period. This March, they wish to make sure that every Filipino citizen begins to take the precautions instructed by the FDP to lower these numbers for the better.
